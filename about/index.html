About

Hi, I'm James.

I'm a PhD candidate investigating the construction of large-scale 3D Morphable Models under Stefanos Zafeiriou in the iBUG group at Imperial College London.

I write a lot of Python code. As part of my PhD I co-founded The Menpo Project with my good friends Patrick Snape, Joan Alabort-i-Medina, and Nontas Antonakos. Menpo is an open source, cross-platform library for training and fitting 2D and 3D deformable models.

I also write a fair amount of Javascript. I developed landmarker.io as part of my PhD, a WebGL-powered 2D and 3D annotation tool.

Outside of my PhD I offer software engineering and software consulting services. You can find out more details about all the projects I have been involved with on my portfolio page, or see my LinkedIn profile for a detailed professional background.

If you think you have an interesting problem that I could help you solve, get in touch.
